Environmental Innovation

Plastic Biodegradation Through Larval Enzymes

The Challenge

Plastic waste represents one of the most persistent environmental problems of our time. Traditional recycling methods are energy-intensive and often inefficient, leaving millions of tons of plastic to accumulate in landfills and ecosystems.

Our Approach

We're investigating the remarkable ability of certain insect larvae and their associated microbiomes to break down plastic polymers. These organisms have evolved enzymes capable of degrading materials that typically resist decomposition for centuries.

Through collaborative research with academic institutions, we're working to:

  • Identify and characterize the specific enzymes and microbial communities responsible for plastic degradation
  • Understand the mechanisms by which these biological systems process different types of plastics
  • Develop scalable methods to harness these capabilities for practical waste management applications
  • Explore the potential for bio-based plastic processing that's more sustainable than conventional approaches

Agricultural Solutions

Enhanced Biological Pest Control Through Insect Nutrition

The Challenge

Biological pest control‚Äîusing beneficial insects to manage agricultural pests‚Äîoffers a sustainable alternative to chemical pesticides. However, the effectiveness of these programs depends heavily on the health, vitality, and reproduction rates of the beneficial insects being reared.

Our Approach

We're developing optimized diets for insects used in biological pest control programs. By understanding the nutritional requirements of these beneficial species, we can improve their rearing efficiency and effectiveness in the field.

Our research focuses on:

  • Analyzing the nutritional needs of key beneficial insect species at different life stages
  • Formulating diets that enhance reproductive success, longevity, and pest-control effectiveness
  • Testing diet formulations to optimize mass-rearing efficiency and reduce costs
  • Developing practical, scalable feeding protocols for commercial insect rearing facilities
